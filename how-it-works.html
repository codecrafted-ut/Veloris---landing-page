The Process

From setup to focus
in under 3 minutes.

Veloris is built around a simple loop: you define your rules, start a session, and the system enforces everything automatically. Here's exactly what happens at each step.

01
Create your account & sign in
Sign in with Google, email, or a magic link — whichever you prefer. Your account is the anchor for your rules, streaks, and analytics. No data is stored on Veloris servers; your productivity data lives locally on your device in a SQLite database.
⚡ Firebase Authentication · Local SQLite · No cloud sync of personal data
02
Define your blocking rules
Choose which apps and websites to block. You can select from a pre-loaded list of 25+ common distractions or add any custom app or domain manually. Set a schedule — block all day, only during work hours, or only when a focus session is active. Rules are saved instantly and take effect immediately.
🚫 Process-level app blocking · DNS-level site blocking · Custom schedules
03
Start a focus session
Hit start on the Pomodoro timer. The moment a session begins, Veloris activates your rules. Blocked apps are force-killed at the process level every 3 seconds — meaning even if they relaunch themselves, Veloris kills them again. Blocked websites are redirected at the system level via the Windows hosts file, bypassing any browser entirely.
⏱ 25 / 5 / 15 min modes · Process killed every 3s · Hosts file blocking
04
The Negotiator intercepts temptation
When you feel the urge to break focus, you open the Distraction Negotiator instead of just disabling the blocker. The AI evaluates your situation — your current streak, your active rules, how long you've been working — and responds accordingly. Sometimes it holds the line. Sometimes it grants a short, timed break and re-locks the app when time expires.
🤖 AI evaluates context · Grants or denies · Timed breaks with auto-relock
05
Build discipline over time
After each session, your analytics update. Streaks are tracked daily. Habits you set in the Discipline system award XP when completed and deduct it when skipped. The XP system, challenges, and level progression aren't just decoration — they give you something concrete to measure your consistency against, day by day.
🔥 Daily streaks · XP system · Level progression · 30-day trend charts
06
Lock it down with Parental PIN (Pro)
For anyone who doesn't trust their future self — or for parents setting up focus mode for their kids — the Parental PIN locks all Veloris settings behind a 4-digit code. No rules can be changed, no features disabled, without entering it first. The session starts, and the system is sealed.
🔒 PIN-locked settings · Pro feature · Zero bypass without code

Process-Level App Blocking
Veloris doesn't just hide apps — it kills their system process. Every 3 seconds, a background loop checks for running processes that match your blocked list and terminates them. This includes launcher processes and update helpers, so apps that auto-relaunch get killed before they can open. Works on any Windows 10/11 machine with no browser extension needed.
SYSTEM-LEVEL
🌐
DNS-Level Website Blocking
Blocked websites are added to the Windows hosts file, which redirects their domains before any browser can load them. This is browser-agnostic — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Brave, all blocked simultaneously with a single rule. No browser extension that can be disabled, no per-browser settings to manage. Requires Administrator privileges to write to the hosts file, which Veloris requests on startup.

The Core Intelligence

The Distraction
Negotiator AI.

Every other productivity tool just blocks you. The Negotiator does something fundamentally different — it meets you in the moment and makes you think.

🤖 Distraction Negotiator

The moment before you give up is the most important moment.

Most people don't disable their blocker because they decided to be unproductive. They disable it because something got hard, or boring, or uncomfortable — and their brain started bargaining. The Negotiator is built specifically for that moment. It doesn't just say no. It talks to you.

The goal isn't to be an obstacle. It's to be the pause your brain needs before a decision that's going to cost you an hour.

How the Negotiator makes its decision

🔥
You trigger it
Instead of disabling your blocks directly, you open the Negotiator. This single step forces a pause — the action you wanted to take now requires a conversation first.
🧠
It reads your context
The Negotiator checks your current streak, your active blocking rules, your stated goals, and how long you've been focused in the current session. It uses all of this to understand whether your request is reasonable or a pattern of avoidance.
💬
It responds with reasoning
The response isn't a generic motivational quote. It's a direct, contextual reply that references what you're actually doing. It might point out that you broke your streak yesterday. It might acknowledge that you've been working for 90 minutes and a break is reasonable. It reasons with you — not at you.
✅
It makes a decision
Based on everything it knows, the Negotiator issues one of three outcomes. Each outcome is intentional — not random, not passive.
🚫
Denied
The block holds. The Negotiator explains why — your streak is at risk, you haven't hit your session goal, or your request pattern suggests avoidance rather than a genuine need. No bypass available.
⏱
Timed Break Granted
A short window is unlocked — typically 5 or 10 minutes. A countdown runs, and when it expires, the block is automatically re-applied. You get the break. You don't get to extend it by forgetting.
✅
Allowed
The Negotiator determines that the request is reasonable — you've been working long enough, your reason is legitimate, or you've earned it. The block is lifted. No guilt. No friction.

The philosophy behind it

Most productivity tools treat distraction as a binary problem: you're either blocked or you're not. Veloris treats it as a behavioral one. The goal isn't to make it impossible to access distracting things — a determined person will always find a way around a blocker. The goal is to make you pause long enough to make a conscious decision instead of a reflexive one.

Over time, that pause becomes a habit. The moment you feel the urge to switch away from work, you'll catch it — not because the app stopped you, but because you've practiced catching it. That's what the Negotiator is actually training: not restriction, but awareness.
